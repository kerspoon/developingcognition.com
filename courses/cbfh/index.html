Misconceptions

  • It is easy to make lasting behaviour change if set up correctly
  • Thinking positively can make things worse
  • Habits don’t take 66 days (or 20)
  • Habits must have 3 parts: a cue, a behaviour and a reward
  • There are two parts to behaviour change -
    • Deciding what to do (selection)
    • Making sure you will do it (implementation)

Task

  1. Have you tried to form a habit without a clear, specific cue? Did it work?
  2. Are you someone who falls into the trap of fantasising about the future without making plans to make it happen?

3. Overview of WOOP

Reflection

  1. How would I summarise the video in one sentence? (do I understand)
  2. How does this fit/differ from what I already know? (why is it true)
  3. Where/how would I use that knowledge? (when is it valuable)

WOOP

  • Wish:
  • Outcome:
  • Obstacle:
  • Plan:

    if

    if

    (situation: where and when)

    then

    then

    (behaviour: action or starting point)

Task

  1. Where have you tried using similar steps? (maybe you already plan out how you will start difficult tasks)

Keys to success with WOOP

  • Start with the positives
  • Be as concrete and specific as possible
  • The stronger the mental links the better
  • Make (if/then) plans to overcome/prevent obstacles as well as for completing tasks

References

     
  1. Rethinking Positive Thinking: Inside the New Science of Motivation - Gabriele Oettingen (October 2014)
    This is a very readable popular science book on over 20 years of academic research into the science of behaviour change. Highly recommended.
    2.  
  2. Chapter 13: Goal Pursuit - Peter Gollwitzer & Gabriele Oettingen in The Oxford Handbook of Human Motivation - Richard Ryan (January 2012)
    An academic overview of the research of goal setting and achievement. A good starting point for academic reading.
    3.  
  3. Chapter 7: Stratagies of Setting and Implementing Goals. Mental contrasting and implementation intentions - Gabriele Oettingen & Peter Gollwitzer in Social psychological foundations of clinical psychology - Richard Ryan (2010)
    Another academic overview, the section on "Setting Goals" is a great condensed literature review on the various ways in which we can maximise the attainment of our goals. It then goes on to discuss MCII in much the same way as [2].
    4.  
  4. Implementation Intentions and Goal Achievement: A Meta-Analysis of Effects and Processes - Peter M. Gollwitzer & Paschal Sheeran in Advances in Experimental Social Psychology (January 2006)
    It's so rare to have a meta-analysis in behaviour change - "94 independent tests showed that implementation intentions had a positive effect of medium‐to‐large magnitude (d = .65)".
