Questions and Answers

What will I actually be doing?

You will be taking part in a series of interactive workshops. That means you will be writing, talking, thinking, meditating and interacting with the other participants.

Some of these workshops will give you the skills to make your goals achieveable. We will be dispelling myths of happiness and giving concrete advice on how to be happier. In each workshop you will have a chance to try out things that are aimed to make your life better.

Is this course right for me?

It doesn't matter what stage of your carrear you are or whether you are into "self-development" or not. As long as you have a desire to change a habit, learn something new or become happier then this is for you.

Collectively we ahve taught everyone from students to corporate businesses. If you are still not sure then fee free to drop us a line at james@developingcognition.com.

Instructors

Dr. James Brooks

Self-development

James is an educator and researcher - sitting between the disciplines of training, education and behavioural nudges. His particular areas of interest are personal effectiveness, decision making, behavioural psychology, communication, and metacognition.

Michael Plant

Happiness

Michael researches the nature and causes of human happiness, working at the intersection of philosophy, psychology and behavioural economics. Michael has founded a happiness consultancy, written about happiness for the New Statesman and the Huffington Post. He’s currently working on a doctorate in philosophy the University of Oxford.

Angelina Castellini

Improv

Angelina is an (applied) improvisation teacher and facilitator, using improvisation techniques for personal and professional development. As part of Paul Jackson Associates, Angelina helped develop and is now a teacher at the Improvisation Academy. She now works with the London School of Economics (LSE) Language Centre to develop Language through Improvisation courses for degree students.

Darius Lau

Mindfulness

Darius is a happiness, well-being and relationship life coach. Having coaching clients across Europe his mission is to make a positive impact across the world. He aims to help people become more self aware allowing them to be conscious in their life enabling to find a purpose in life. His interests include meditation, energy work, nutrition, biohacking, improving mental performance and conscious parenting.

Sam Hilton

Altruism

Sam is currently both a policy advisor at the UK Treasury and a charity entrepreneur who is currently running the Effective Altruism London community. This involves regularly hosting workshops and talks on topics such as ethical self improvement and effective ways to make the world better. Sam has a strong interest in evidence based self-improvement and has recently started formally teaching self-improvement and personal productivity skills.
