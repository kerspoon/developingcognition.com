Levelling Up 2016

A Full Day of Self-Development Workshops

Developing Cognition are hosting a day of workshops on self-development. Questions and Answers

What will I actually be doing?

This course is meant to be practical so, in addition to learning about the subjects we will give you techniques to improve happiness, create strong habits, meditate, and communicate better.

For example in the "Achieving Goals" workshop you will learn the best tools from the last two decades of research into goals and habits, then we will each practice using those tools to turn vague aims like "get fitter" into a strong action plan that you are likely to stick to.


Is this course right for me?

It doesn't matter what stage of your career you are or whether you are into "self-development" or not. As long as you have a desire to change a habit, learn something new or become happier then this is for you.

Collectively we have taught everyone from students to corporate businesses. If you are still not sure then feel free to drop us a line at james@developingcognition.com.

What will I get out of it?

Each workshop will give you something different. In each you will have a chance to understand where and when to use these skills and practice using them. The instructors will be available all day to answer your questions and help you through you roadblocks.

  1. Concrete knowledge and skills of how to improve your happiness, communication, and habits.
  2. Practice creating an action plan to make new year's resolutions stick.
  3. A chance to try mindfulness meditation, and visualisation techniques.

Instructors

Dr. James Brooks

Self-development

James is an educator and researcher - sitting between the disciplines of training, education and behavioural nudges. His particular areas of interest are personal effectiveness, decision making, behavioural psychology, communication, and metacognition.

Michael Plant

Happiness

Michael researches the nature and causes of human happiness, working at the intersection of philosophy, psychology and behavioural economics. Michael has founded a happiness consultancy, written about happiness for the New Statesman and the Huffington Post. He’s currently working on a doctorate in philosophy the University of Oxford.

Angelina Castellini

Improv

Angelina is an (applied) improvisation teacher and facilitator, using improvisation techniques for personal and professional development. As part of Paul Jackson Associates, Angelina helped develop and is a teacher at the Improvisation Academy. Darius Lau

Sam Hilton
